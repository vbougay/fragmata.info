**After 20 months below the line, Cyprus's 18 main reservoirs have crossed 100 million cubic metres of stored water — a milestone that seemed unthinkable when the season opened at barely 27 MCM in late December.**

---

On 7 April 2026, total storage in Cyprus's 18 main dams reached **100.2 MCM (34.4%)**, up from 98.7 MCM (33.9%) just yesterday. The last time reserves sat above 100 MCM was early August 2024 — roughly 20 months ago. Everything between then and now was a long, painful slide: by late December 2025, the island had dipped to **26.9 MCM (9.2%)**, the lowest point in years.

The turnaround has been remarkable.

### The Climb Back

| Date | Storage (MCM) | Fill |
|------|--------------|------|
| 29 Dec 2025 | 26.9 | 9.2% |
| 16 Feb 2026 | 50.0 | 17.2% |
| 23 Mar 2026 | 78.1 | 26.9% |
| 31 Mar 2026 | 91.7 | 31.5% |
| 6 Apr 2026 | 98.7 | 33.9% |
| **7 Apr 2026** | **100.2** | **34.4%** |

Seasonal inflow since October now stands at **82.5 MCM** — 4.4 times the entire 2024/25 drought year total of 18.7 MCM. February and March together delivered over 59 MCM, rewriting the season's trajectory from crisis to recovery.

### Where the Water Went

The gains are widespread. Eight dams are overflowing at 100% capacity — [Xyliatos](/dam/xyliatos/), [Kalopanagiotis](/dam/kalopanagiotis/), [Argaka](/dam/argaka/), [Pomos](/dam/pomos/), [Agia Marina](/dam/agia-marina/), [Klirou-Malounta](/dam/klirou-malounta/), [Solea](/dam/solea/), and [Tamassos](/dam/tamassos/) (near capacity at 99.5%).

The big dams are the ones that moved the needle:

- **[Kouris](/dam/kouris/)** — 31.9% (36.7 MCM), up from 19.5% last year. New season high, with 14.0 MCM pumped in from [Arminou](/dam/arminou/)
- **[Asprokremmos](/dam/asprokremmos/)** — 36.3% (19.0 MCM), up from 24.8% last year
- **[Evretou](/dam/evretou/)** — 42.2% (10.1 MCM), up from 24.5% last year
- **[Kannaviou](/dam/kannaviou/)** — 46.4% (8.0 MCM), up from 26.0% last year

Together, these four hold 73.8 MCM — nearly three quarters of the island's total reserves.

### Not There Yet

The milestone is real, but the picture is incomplete. Several dams remain far below healthy levels:

- **[Achna](/dam/achna/)** — 2.1%, zero inflow all season
- **[Kalavasos](/dam/kalavasos/)** — 15.1%, being [deliberately drained](/articles/2026-04-03-kalavasos-southern-conveyor/) to supply three districts
- **[Lefkara](/dam/lefkara/)** — 15.8%, well below last year's 26.6%

The island-wide 10% water supply cut remains in effect. At 34.4%, reserves are still well below the long-term April average. Officials have repeatedly stressed that one good season does not erase the structural water deficit built up over four dry years.

But today, for the first time in almost two years, there are 100 million cubic metres of water in Cyprus's dams. That's worth marking.
